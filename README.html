RADARIS

Advanced Radio-Frequency Hazard Detection System for Enhanced Road Safety

I. Abstract

The core purpose of this study is the development of a hazard warning system for frontal collisions during limited visibility to drastically reduce road accidents. Traditional Advanced Driver Assistance Systems (ADAS) are often compromised by weather particulates like fog, rain, or snow. The Radaris system utilizes radio waves that penetrate these elements, ensuring effective prevention regardless of climatic or road conditions.

Scientific Novelty

Radaris introduces a terrain-independent approach to collision prevention. By integrating autonomous radio-frequency communication into vehicle safety stacks, it creates a "digital sight" that functions where human vision and cameras fail.

Research Methods

The following methods were utilized:
  • Empirical research of scientific materials.
  • Experimental testing of NRF transmission.
  • Modeling of collision scenarios.
  • Analytical processing of operation results.

III. Technical Architecture

Radaris operates on the 2.4 GHz Industrial, Scientific, and Medical (ISM) band. The system architecture is designed for low latency and high reliability.

Hardware Stack

  • Main Controller ATmega 328
  • Radio Module NRF24L01+
  • Frequency Band 2.4 GHz
  • Output Device LCD & High-Decibel Buzzer
  • Antenna System 90cm Copper / 1.0mm Dia.

Safety Performance

While a vehicle at 100 km/h requires a 475m braking distance, Radaris provides a 900m detection range. This provides the driver with nearly double the required time to react to a frontal hazard.

Key Features:
  • Weather independence (Fog/Snow/Rain).
  • Radio wave safety (Non-hazardous to health).
  • Wide application across all road surfaces.
